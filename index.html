Research & papers
Paper 1 · Econometrics & Policy
Economic Diversification in Saudi Arabia: The Role of Oil Rents, FDI, and Vision 2030
University of Kent · March 2026
Examines the impact of oil rents, FDI, and the Vision 2030 reform programme on economic diversification in Saudi Arabia (1990–2021) using time-series econometrics — OLS with HAC (Newey-West) standard errors, interaction analysis, and IV estimation via 2SLS. Finds oil rents support the "resource blessing" hypothesis; Vision 2030 produced a significant positive level shift; FDI remains statistically insignificant across all specifications.
Oil rents
+0.141 units diversification (p < 0.01)
Vision 2030
+1.895 unit level shift (p < 0.05)
FDI result

Paper 2 · Environmental Economics
Internalizing Externalities: An Analysis of Pigovian Taxes and Environmental Policy Failures
ECON7007 — Sustainable Growth & Environmental Valuation · University of Kent · May 2026
Examines the theoretical foundations and practical limitations of Pigovian taxation through three comparative case studies: Ireland's plastic bag levy (success), Spain's Royal Decree 900/2015 "Sun Tax" (failure), and London's evolving congestion and emission charge regime (mixed). Identifies recurring causes of policy failure and proposes design principles for effective environmental taxation.
